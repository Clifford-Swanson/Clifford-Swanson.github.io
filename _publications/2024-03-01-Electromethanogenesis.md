Electro-methanogenic microbial communities can producebiogaswith high efficiency and have attracted extensive research interest. In this study an alternating polarity strategy was developed to build electro-methanogenic communities. In two-chamber bioelectrochemical systems amended with activated carbon, the electrode potential was alternated between+0.8V and ?0.4V vs. standard hydrogen electrode every three days. Cumulativebiogasproduction under alternating polarity increased from 45 L/L/kg-activated carbon after start-up to 125 L/L/kg after the 4th enrichment, significantly higher than that under intermittent cathode (?0.4V/open circuit), continuous cathode (?0.4V), and open circuit. The communities assembled under alternating polarity were electroactive and structurally different from those assembled under other conditions. OneMethanobacteriumpopulation and twoGeobacterpopulations were consistently abundant and active in the communities. Their 16S rRNA was up-regulated by electrode potentials. Bayesian networks inferred close associations between these populations. Overall, electro-methanogenic communities have been successfully assembled with alternating polarity.
